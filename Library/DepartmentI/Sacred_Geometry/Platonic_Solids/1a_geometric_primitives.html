The $\mathbb{R}^3$ Coordinate Space

Before the S.P.I.R.E. Engine can instantiate an idea, it requires a dimensional theater. The fundamental canvas of our objective reality is $\mathbb{R}^3$ space. It is an infinite, continuous set of coordinates mapped across three orthogonal axes.

Mathematically, $\mathbb{R}^3$ is defined as a vector space over real numbers where every spatial singularity (a point) is defined by an exact tuple:

$$\mathbb{R}^3 = \{ (x, y, z) \mid x, y, z \in \mathbb{R} \}$$

Simply Put: The 3D Room

Imagine a giant, empty room. To pinpoint exactly where a fly is hovering in that room, you need three measurements:
1. How far left/right (the X axis).
2. How high up/down (the Y axis).
3. How far forward/backward (the Z axis).
This 3D grid is what mathematicians call $\mathbb{R}^3$ space. The tuple $(x, y, z)$ is just the fly's GPS address.

Within this void, we establish order through Geometric Primitives. These are the ascending dimensional constructs that bridge the gap between nothingness (0D) and volumetric existence (3D). The absolute Euclidean distance between any two anchors $A(x_1, y_1, z_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2, z_2)$ forms the logic vectors of the Engine:

$$d(A,B) = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2 + (z_2 - z_1)^2}$$

Simply Put: The 3D Ruler

This scary-looking math formula is just the famous Pythagorean theorem ($A^2 + B^2 = C^2$) upgraded for 3D space. If you want to know the length of a straight line connecting two dots in space, this formula acts as your digital ruler.

The Dimensional Ascent

0D: Vertex

Dim: 0

A singularity in the manifold. It has no length, width, or depth; only a definitive spatial coordinate $(x, y, z)$. It is the anchor of intent.

Simply Put: A single dot. It has no size, it just marks a specific location.

1D: Edge

Dim: 1

The shortest path binding two vertices. An edge possesses length but lacks area or volume. It represents a logical vector relationship.

Simply Put: A line connecting two dots. You can measure how long it is, but it has no width.

2D: Face

Dim: 2

A planar surface enclosed by three or more edges. Faces establish a boundary condition, creating an internal and external context.

Simply Put: A flat shape, like a sheet of paper. It has surface area, but no thickness.

3D: Volume

Dim: 3

The encapsulated space bound by multiple intersecting faces. The engine utilizes complete volumetric solids to store uncorrupted data states.

Simply Put: A solid object, like a box. It takes up real space and you can put things inside it.
