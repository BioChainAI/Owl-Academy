The Geometric Trojan Horse

Deconstructing recursive verification failures within the 12-Nested $64$-Cube protocol.

In physical networks, data is evaluated by examining its constituent bytes, headers, or hashes. However, in advanced coordinate projective structures, packets are evaluated as pure spatial geometry. Packets are projected into hyper-dimensional space to verify their mathematical resonance before they are permitted passage.

"A vessel may appear perfectly balanced upon its outer surface, yet carry a structural defect hidden within its deepest folds. If the harbor master only inspects the outer hull, the vessel enters—but once the cargo is opened at the docks, the unstable geometry tears the local landscape." — Master Scribe's Log, Epoch IV

1. The Geometry of a Sieve

When a packet is compiled, it is mapped into a coordinate space through a projection function $\Phi$:

$$\Phi : \mathbb{Z} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{24}$$

To determine if a packet is safe, the network consensus routes its coordinates through an $\mathbb{R}^{24}$ **Lattice Prime Sieve**. The coordinates must avoid collision zones defined by forbidden prime-modular residue boundaries:

$$\text{Valid State} \Longleftrightarrow M \pmod{p_i} \neq 0$$

If the outer coordinate satisfies this modular criterion, the packet is admitted. However, an exploit lies in the discrepancy between the outer envelope and the nested interior payload.

2. The Trojan Mechanism: Sieve Validation Failure

The vulnerability is characterized as a **topological deficit**. The outer shape claims to contain a normal, balanced structure, but deep nested layers hold recursive coordinate deficits.

$$\kappa(\text{Outer}) = \text{stable (Validation Bypass)}$$

$$\kappa(\text{Inner}) \pmod{p_{coll}} = 0 \quad \text{(Malicious Structural Collapse)}$$

If the consensus network only verifies up to depth $d < 7$, a Trojan hidden at depth $d = 7$ passes the global checks cleanly. Once it is received by a local node, the node unwraps the entire payload recursively:

$$f(d) = \sum_{v=1}^{64} \text{process}(v, f(d+1))$$
