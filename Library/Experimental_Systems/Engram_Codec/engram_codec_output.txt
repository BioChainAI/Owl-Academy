FINDINGS (measured):
 • SECTIONS recreate EXACTLY. Block-boundary cumulative state telescopes to ~1e-15,
   independent of the data — so 'reliably recreate sections' is literally true at the
   section (block) level, for ANY stream. That is the real, usable guarantee.
 • INTRA-section detail is LOSSY and data-dependent. Smooth/structured streams
   reconstruct with tiny RMSE even at 60× compression; a random-walk degrades
   gracefully; high-entropy data is essentially destroyed (RMSE ≈ its own spread).
   This is information theory, not a flaw: you cannot losslessly compress randomness,
   and equal-share SLERP keeps only each block's net rotation.
 • Smallest-Three quantization is stable (bounded round-trip error), as proved.

USE IT FOR: smooth embeddings, sensor/trajectory streams, slowly-varying context —
where it is a genuine multi-resolution memory. NOT a general lossless store.
