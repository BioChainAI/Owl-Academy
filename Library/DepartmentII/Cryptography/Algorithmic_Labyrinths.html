Algorithmic Labyrinths

The Avalanche Effect & Hash Function Design

Manuscript In Progress

Course Overview

A well-constructed cryptographic primitive behaves as a labyrinth: flipping one input bit cascades through the internal state until roughly half the output bits change — the avalanche effect. This tome examines the diffusion-and-confusion machinery of modern block ciphers and hash functions, with particular attention to the design choices that produce statistical unpredictability from purely deterministic operations.

Planned Sections

  • ⬡ Shannon's confusion and diffusion principles
  • ⬡ Substitution-permutation networks (AES)
  • ⬡ Feistel networks and their structural symmetries
  • ⬡ The strict avalanche criterion (SAC) and bit-independence
  • ⬡ Merkle-Damgård vs. sponge constructions (SHA-2, SHA-3)
  • ⬡ Differential and linear cryptanalysis — measuring the labyrinth
  • ⬡ Application: hash-based commitment schemes in SHD-CCP
